# Comment 32: Notational slip in describing Huisman and Kort (2015)

**Referee's claim**: Using β_H to denote the characteristic root in the Huisman and Kort (2015) framework causes confusion with this paper's own β_H (H-regime root).

## Assessment

**Relevant**: Yes, but minor. The notation overloading is understandable in context but could confuse.

**Needs addressing**: Yes, with a small notation change.

## Analysis

The paper writes: "F(X) ∝ X^{β_H} is strictly convex" when describing the Huisman and Kort framework. Here β_H is used generically to denote "the positive characteristic root," but in this paper β_H specifically denotes the H-regime root, which has a particular value (≈1.55) and economic meaning.

The Huisman and Kort framework uses a standard GBM without regime switching, so their characteristic root would typically be denoted β or β^+ in textbooks.

## Suggested Fixes

1. **Use generic notation**: "In their model, L(X) is approximately affine in X while F(X) ∝ X^β (where β > 1 is the positive characteristic root of the standard GBM problem) is strictly convex with F(0) = 0 > L(0), yielding at most one crossing."
